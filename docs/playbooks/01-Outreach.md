# Playbook 01: Dealership Outreach (WhatsApp + Email)
Goal: start conversations with serious dealerships and book a decision-maker call.

## Golden rules
- No long paragraphs
- One clear reason for contact
- One proof point
- One next step (book a call)
- Never ask for passwords
- Always offer value first

## Before outreach (5 minutes)
Collect:
- Dealership name + branch location
- Decision maker name (sales manager, GM, ops manager)
- Website link
- Google Business Profile link
- 1 visible issue or opportunity (booking flow, deposit clarity, missing trust signals, slow forms)

Create:
- 3 bullet "Opportunity Notes"
- 1 sentence "Reason for contact"

## Offer options (pick ONE)
A) Deposit + reservation system with audit trail and receipts
B) Secure customer portal (service booking, photo uploads, status updates)
C) Vehicle Passport (docs, media, handover evidence, dispute protection)

## WhatsApp first message (short)
Hi <Name>, Jeshmeer here from Sovereign Compliance Systems.

I reviewed <Dealership> online presence and saw 2 opportunities:
1) <one line>
2) <one line>

We build dealership systems like:
- reservation + deposit workflows
- evidence grade audit trails and exports
- customer portals

If I send a 1 page mini audit + a few screenshots of a demo build, who is the right person to show it to?

## If they ask: "Why did you visit our website?"
Use one of these:
- "I only checked what any customer sees. I was mapping how clients reserve and how deposits are recorded."
- "I am researching the top dealerships in Dubai to understand real workflows, then I approach only the best fit."
- "I focus on dispute prevention and audit trails. Your site showed a strong brand, so I checked the booking and deposit flow."

## Follow up sequence (WhatsApp)
### Follow up 1 (24 hours)
Hi <Name>, quick one.
Should I send the mini audit to you or to your GM / sales manager?

### Follow up 2 (3 days)
Hi <Name>, I can show you a 10 minute demo of:
- deposit receipt
- audit trail timeline
- evidence export pack

What is better, <day/time option 1> or <day/time option 2>?

### Follow up 3 (7 days)
Hi <Name>, last message from me.
If you are not the right contact, just point me to the correct person and I will be brief.

## Call booking line
"I only need 15 minutes. If it is not a fit, you will still leave with a clear plan."

## What you send as the mini audit (structure)
- 3 opportunity notes
- 3 risk notes (disputes, lost leads, missing proof)
- 1 recommended MVP (one of A/B/C)
- 1 timeline (2 to 4 weeks)
- 1 pricing range or "from" anchor (premium positioning)

## Red flags (do not proceed)
- They refuse deposit
- They cannot name a decision maker
- They want everything in phase 1
- They ask for your full pricing list with no call

## Green flags (push forward)
- They mention disputes, refunds, approvals, missing proof
- They mention staff time wasted
- They have a premium brand and want control
- They ask about audit trails and evidence exports
