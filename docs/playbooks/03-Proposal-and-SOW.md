# Playbook 03: Proposal and SOW (Scope + Money Protection)

Goal: close the deal with clear scope, milestones, and change control.

## What you deliver (in the proposal)

1. Problem summary (their words)
2. MVP outcome (30 day target)
3. Scope (5 features max)
4. Timeline (2 to 6 weeks)
5. Milestones + payment schedule
6. Assumptions and exclusions
7. Change request process
8. Support options after launch

### 4) Timeline

Week 1: prototype + data model + audit foundation
Week 2: core workflow + admin controls
Week 3: evidence vault + exports + notifications
Week 4: hardening + training + launch

### 5) Milestones and payments

- Milestone 1: Discovery confirmation + prototype sign off (deposit)
- Milestone 2: Core workflow delivered to staging
- Milestone 3: Evidence exports and audit reporting delivered
- Milestone 4: Launch and handover complete

### 6) Acceptance

Acceptance is based on:

- MVP scope checklist passed
- UAT sign off email from <Decision Maker>
- Production launch checklist completed

### 7) Change requests

Any request outside MVP scope:

- is written as a Change Request
- has price and impact stated
- starts only after approval

### 8) Support after launch

Option A: Essential (monitoring + updates)
Option B: Priority (faster response + monthly improvements)
Option C: Executive (governance pack updates + roadmap planning)

## Pricing anchor (premium positioning)

Use one of these patterns:

- "Projects from AED <x> with clear milestones"
- "MVP build is AED <x> to <y> depending on integrations"
- "Monthly support starts from AED <x>"

Do not discount early.
Instead:

- reduce scope
- keep quality and governance standard

## SOW essentials (minimum clauses)

Include:

- Scope and exclusions
- Timeline and dependencies
- Payment terms
- Change control
- IP and license terms
- Confidentiality
- Warranty window (example: 14 or 30 days)
- Support boundaries
- Data handling summary (high level)

## Assumptions list (protect you)

Examples:

- "Client provides branding and legal receipt details within 3 business days"
- "Payment provider supports required workflows"
- "Any third party integration depends on API availability"
- "Client assigns one decision maker for sign offs"

## If they push for everything

Say:
"We can do it, but not in phase 1.
Phase 1 is the smallest version that moves money, reduces disputes, and saves staff time.
Phase 2 is the add ons."

## Close line

"If you confirm the MVP scope today, I will send the SOW and milestone 1 invoice, then we start with the clickable prototype."
